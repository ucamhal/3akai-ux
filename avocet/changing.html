What's changing?

HEFCE has a new policy for open access in the next Research Excellence Framework (REF).

HEFCE policy requires you to upload your accepted, peer-reviewed articles and conference proceedings to an institutional repository.

RCUK, Wellcome and other funders also have various policies relating to the open access and licensing of your research.

Upload your accepted manuscripts to meet this requirement and make sure you are compliant with the REF, your funder’s and publisher’s open access policies.

What type of manuscripts are affected?

All accepted journal articles and conference proceedings (with an SSIN) should be uploaded, to ensure they are eligible for the next REF.

It does not apply to monographs, book chapters or other long-form publications, working papers, creative or practice-based research outputs or data.

When do I need to do something?

As soon as your peer reviewed manuscript has been accepted upload it here.

HEFCE policy requires manuscripts to be uploaded no later than three months after acceptance.

This policy comes into force on 1 April 2016. Yet at the University we want to ensure this shift is managed sooner rather than later to ensure no research is omitted.

When you upload your manuscript you will be guided through the process, making it as simple and clear as possible.

Eligibility for the REF

By uploading your peer-reviewed manuscript as soon as it is accepted by a publisher, you adhere to HEFCE policy.

This is the easiest way of letting us know you are being published.

We can help you to ensure your REF eligibility.

Failure to do so could affect your access to future funding.

Publisher embargoes are respected (the period when you can only access an article with a paid access). Manuscripts must be free to read and download after the following periods:

  • 12 months for STEM subjects
  • 24 months for Humanities subjects

Other Funder Requirements

Funders have a range of open access policies, licensing and copyright requirements.

To make this process is simple as possible for you, when you upload your manuscript we’ll ask for the details of those who funded your research.

Therefore we can ensure you adhere to their requirements.

RCUK

From 1 April 2013 all articles arising from research funded by RCUK must be made available under open access, in line with government policy.

Read more about the RCUK open access policy

The Wellcome Trust

Wellcome Trust Grant Conditions require that all research papers funded in whole or in part through the Europe PubMed Central (Europe PMC) or PubMed Central (PMC) should be available with an electronic copy in a repository as soon as possible or within six months of publication. This applies to all research papers that have been submitted and accepted for publication in a peer reviewed journal.
