What do I need to do?

When a journal accepts your manuscript for publication you need to upload it.

Which manuscripts do I need to upload?

Upload articles or conference proceedings (with an ISSN). Monographs, books and other book chapters do not need to be uploaded.

Which version should I upload?

You should upload your Accepted Author Manuscript. This is your version of the manuscript after peer review and including any final changes, but it is likely not to have been typeset or copy-edited by a publisher.

What formats do you accept?

We accept any file format including Word, Pages and PDF. We will ensure each manuscript uploaded to the repository is converted to a PDF file.

We are currently only able to accept one file per upload. For example, a manuscript in LaTeX format is comprised of multiple files, therefore a LaTeX file will need to be converted to a PDF before it can be uploaded. We hope to be able to accept multiple files for one article in future.

I need to pay a publisher’s fee

If you have already opted for a “pay to publish” model you may need to pay an Article Processing Charge, or APC. Depending on the funder, the University might support you in covering this publishing fee.

Open access funds are available at the University, funded by RCUK and The Wellcome Trust. Both funding bodies have set conditions linked to the use of a publisher’s fee.

For RCUK funded articles that are compliant with RCUK open access policies, the University can pay for colour and page charges. The University cannot pay for other funders’ colour, page and image charges; these are the responsibility of the author.

If you have any queries or require guidance then please contact us.

Copyright

When you return your edited manuscript to your publisher you are asked to sign a Copyright Transfer Agreement. This is standard in most traditional publication agreements. It is important for you to retain rights as an author enabling you to publish your work open access, use it in teaching materials and to promote your research work i.e., via your website.

Why might you want to retain copyright?

A traditional publisher’s agreement, which sees you transfer copyright to the publisher, might affect your eligibility for the next REF.

Retaining copyright, or adding an addendum which allows you to comply with funders’ open access policies, will ensure your work is eligible. It will also help you reach a wider audience and have more control over how the distribution of your work online.

Your Copyright Options

Your Accepted Author Manuscript will be uploaded to the University of Cambridge (University) repository and will be embargoed until at least the publication date. Unless you agree otherwise, your manuscript will not be open access immediately. Instead we will contact you to confirm how you would like us to proceed once the publisher’s embargo period has ended. If we cannot contact you we will publish your manuscript in accordance with the funder policy to ensure it is eligible for REF.

The University will not publish, distribute or modify your work until after the funder’s embargo expires without your permission.

If you have any questions or need clarification please contact us.

You have two copyright options;

1. Use an Author Addendum

The SPARC Author Addendum enables you to change the traditional agreement you have with your publisher to allow you to retain key rights in relation to your work. You still transfer copyright, but include a caveat covering digital usage and access.

If you need advice and guidance on how to approach your publisher for an addendum to your existing agreement please contact us.

2. Sign a traditional copyright agreement

A CTA (Copyright Transfer Agreement) will give your publisher copyright but might affect how and when you can make your manuscript open access.

To make sure you work is still eligible for the REF we’ll check your publisher’s model for licensing and open access.

Your rights will vary depending on your publisher but if you sign a CTA you may need to request permission to;

  • Upload your manuscript to the University repository
  • Publish your manuscript on your website
  • Share your manuscript on Academia.edu and Research Gate
  • Distribute your article to colleagues and students
  • Include your articles in course materials
  • Make modifications to your article

If you would like us to check your publisher’s CTA and offer you help and guidance on your rights then please contact us.

What to do if you’ve already signed an agreement

If you have already entered into a traditional publisher’s contract but may need to arrange a retrospective amendment.

If your publisher does not offer a Gold open access model then your manuscript may not be eligible for the next REF.

You may need to request permission from your publisher retrospectively if you need to;

  • Upload your manuscript to the University repository
  • Publish your manuscript on your website
  • Share your manuscript on Academia.edu and Research Gate
  • Distribute your article to colleagues and students
  • Include your articles in course materials
  • Make modifications to your article

We can liaise with your publisher and help you to ensure you’re both REF and funder compliant. If your article is not yet published we can support you in negotiation for amended rights with your publisher. We may, or may not be able to upload your manuscript in the repository. If we can, we will cite the journal as copyright holder.

Licensing

As well as copyright, different funders and publishers adhere to a range of licensing agreements. Licenses affect how your work can be copied, distributed, shared and how others can use it, commercially and non-commercially.

CC BY

This license lets others distribute, remix, tweak and build upon your work, including commercially, as long as they credit you for the original creation.

CC BY-NC

This license lets others remix, tweak, and build upon your work non-commercially, and although their new works must also acknowledge you and be non-commercial, they don’t have to license their derivative works on the same terms.

CC BY-NC-ND

This license is the most restrictive, only allowing others to download your works and share them with others as long as they credit you, but they can’t change them in any way or use them commercially.

Acknowledge your funder

You will need to acknowledge your funder when your manuscript is published.

The Research Information Network has developed a standard acknowledgment which is acceptable to all funders. Acknowledgements should follow these guidelines;

  • the funding agency name should be written in full
  • the grant number should be in square brackets
  • multiple grant numbers should be separated by a comma and space
  • funding agencies should be separated by a semi-colon.

eg. 'This work was supported by the Wellcome Trust [grant number], [grant number]; Medical Research Council [grant number] and Cancer Research UK [grant number]
